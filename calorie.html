Summary

How Local Businesses will operate in the future?

An app where local businesses sell their goods via a messaging app and create their own store on the platform for better customer discovery ,service and cost effective business. Digitising the way local business is done offline.

Deliverables

  • Problem Solution Hypothesis.
  • User Research.
  • Wire Framing.
  • Visual Design.
  • Branding.

    • Problem

  • 1. The premise for making such an app came from my need as an avid WhatsApp business user to implement search and directory based information within WhatsApp.
  • 2. WhatsAppcannot be used as a marketing tool due to the lack of analytics.
  • 3. Local businesses sell via trust.
  • 4. Local Business owners are not well worsed with Tech.
  • 5. WhatsApp sitrignet rules.
  • 6. Local businesses have to store inventory.
  • 7. Local business startup capital is relatively high.

    • Solution

  • 1. The main aim to make this design is to solve a real world problem of how local business can market themselves to the costumers that trust them without storing any form of inventory.
  • 2. Create easy to user interaction similar to WhatsApp which local business can use to setup shop listing every product directly from the distributor.
  • 3. Help new local businesses start at a very low investment.
  • 4. Help improve local businesses reach through referrals from happy customers.

    • Design to Solve

    There is no main stream platform built for local business owners with lack of technical skills to setup shop and sell to customer with low investment on inventory.

    Designing for Trust

    India being a large local market and everything sold is on the basis of trust and marketing in the local business space is foreign.

    Designing for analytics

    Marketing is something that should be accessible to every local business owner.

    Design to Build Relationships

    Converting a consumer who has already used you product is critical, which can be done through customer service which the local businesses lack.

    Designing for Ease of Use

    Simplicity and Familiarity are the main pillars focus on in this project. Less animations and fancy ui.

    User Research

    Statistics

  • 1. 90% said they shop locally
  • 2. 25% business own websites.
  • 3. 75% users said they would love shopping local products online.
  • 4. 71% business owners say that are proficient with using WhatsApp
  • 5. 53% buyers said they shop first copy goods on WhatsApp.
  • 6. 70% businesses say they have returning customers and 68% business owners used UPI and G pay.

    • Branding

    Logo Breakdown

  • The logo is inspired from the ancient Trinity Knot Celtic card which is a symbol of trust. It symbolises the Earth, Air ,Water, Mind Body Soul, Past ,Present and Future.
  • The name “tera” symbolises being in harmony with the customers needs and everything done is to solve the customer’s problems and make the end users life better.

    • Color Scheme

  • Trust is the building block in local businesses so this logo would be most relevant.Similarly the colour chosen is a Blue which symbolises trust between the customer and seller.

    • Wireframing

  • I always start with sketching out on paper and taking notes, that way I can easily see if what’s in my mind makes sense at all without taking up too much time to wireframe it just to realize what I envisioned won’t work. Pen and paper are still pretty useful, even in 2019… Who would’ve thought?

    • Wireframes

  • After sketches come simple wireframes that are a cleaner, on screen representation of my sketches which I use for reference while creating the actual UI. This also helps a lot since I can map out the whole user flow and see if the UX is good already, and after all of that comes the most fun part out of all, the UI design. But first, let’s get some inspiration…

    • Key Components:

  • These are the key components of the UI, I made these such that the MVP could be used by a first users and then could be improved after getting user feedback

    • Chats

  • 1. Chats is the section where you can chat with any of your contacts.
  • 2. Negotiate with any local business
  • 3. Refer Products to friends and family and earn rewards.
  • 4. Make payments to local businesses
  • 5. Review Delivery Options

    • Offers

  • 1. Offers are where local businesses can post offers like stories.
  • 2. Such offers can be shared by customers to gain rewards.
  • 3. Winning Products can be shared.
  • 4. Exclusivity is provided to customers that follow the seller.

    • Directory

  • 1. Directory is where users can quickly search sellers and local businesses
  • 2. The place where local businesses can set up shop.
  • 3. Can list their products and manage inventory.
  • 4. Network with local distributors.

    • Product Page

  • A simple page where a customer can find product details, reviews ,descriptions and negotiate with the seller via chat.Local businesses in India have always had some form of negotiation involved in the process of buying products.

    • Setup Shop

  • A simple interface for businesses to setup their shop and list products they want to sell to consumers.

    • Profile Page:

  • A place where the either the business owner or customer can create a captivating profile.
